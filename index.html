Yeah... that's pretty much what I thought. So I looked online and found a place that guaranteed the lowest price, it sounded promising. I shipped my HDD to them and after a bit they sent it back with an estimate attached, and a pre-paid shipping label if I decided to have them recover the data. Their estimate was right around $6,000. Yes, that is how much Data Recovery cost, and by Data Recovery I hope you're not naive to infer that Data Recovery is,

“OH NO! I deleted a really important file, AND emptied my Recycle Bin, ANNDDD *nervous flop sweat* then I defragged my HDD, it's gone forever.”

...if that is you, then you don't deserve to step foot in the building of Computing and Engineering Sciences. Anyways, I got interested in the subject of Data Recovery, mainly due its cost, I figured the employees must make a pretty damn good salary. Now here's the thing, probably 60%-70% of HDD that actually do seem like they need Data Recovery can actually be recovered using software, if it's worse you can actually replace the PCB on HDDs which solve a lot of errors that would seem unfixable. Data Recovery is ONLY needed when the HDD has to be physically opened. I came to find out the cost comes from the fact that when, in the slim chance, Data Recovery is the only option it requires a Level 3 Lab, that is sterile and particle free, full body suits are worn, and it takes days of meticulous labor to fix. A single particle of dust hitting the read/write head is like a car slamming head-on into a tree. Unfortunately Data Recovery isn't really one of the things you can go to school for, which I found a little hard to believe, but it still seems to be true. From There the story really wraps up quick. I realized that Data Recovery not really being an easy to enter career, I kind of shifted my viewpoint, not as you may think either. I figured if someone could write some code that could do something to my HDD that would result in a $6,000 min fix, I wanted to learn to do it to. As is with many things in life, in order to have the knowledge to destroy, one must have the knowledge to create.
